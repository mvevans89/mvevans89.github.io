Hi! I'm Michelle.

I'm a PhD student in the Integrative Conservation
and Ecology program at the University of Georgia,
advised by Dr. John Drake and Dr. Courtney Murdock.

I am interested in the effects of urbanization on vector-borne disease dynamics and ecology. Specifically, my research is focused on how mosquito community ecology scales-up across a landscape to influence human risk of mosquito-borne disease. I approach this problem from an interdisciplinary perspective, incorporating the social, ecological, and political complexities characteristic of urban systems.

Current Projects

Urban microclimate and mosquito-borne disease

How does microclimate across an urban gradient alter disease dynamics of dengue in Aedes albopictus?

Context-dependent competition between mosquito species

Are the effects of interspecific competition between Aedes aegypti and Anopheles stephensi dependent on temperature?

Social inequalities and disease burdens

How are inequalities in access to water manifest in spatial patterns of vector and water-borne disease burdens?
